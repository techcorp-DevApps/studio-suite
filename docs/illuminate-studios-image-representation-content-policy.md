# Illuminate Studios — Image Representation and Content Policy

## Purpose

This policy applies to all client-facing and internal written content created for Illuminate Studios, including website copy, package descriptions, booking content, gallery introductions, marketing materials, automated messages, social content, contracts, FAQs, and in-application text.

Its purpose is to ensure that all content accurately represents how clients appear in their photographs and avoids language that implies digital alteration of a person’s appearance.

## 1. Image Editing, Retouching and Appearance-Adjustment Language Is Strictly Forbidden

Content must never use terms, references, or wording that directly or indirectly implies that client images are edited, retouched, corrected, adjusted, enhanced, altered, reshaped, softened, filtered, manipulated, polished, or modified after capture.

This applies especially to boudoir-style, portrait, family, maternity, birthing, branding, and personal-confidence sessions.

The policy applies regardless of whether the wording appears in:

- Package descriptions
- Website pages
- Gallery copy
- Booking flows
- Email templates
- Social media content
- Advertisements
- FAQs
- Client guides
- Promotional campaigns
- Internal scripts or templates

## 2. Required Positioning

All content must position a person’s appearance in photographs as natural and straight out of camera.

Approved framing includes:

> The images reflect how each individual appears in camera, with the session focused on lighting, posing, comfort, expression, and professional capture.

> Sessions are guided with careful lighting, natural posing, and considered direction to create photographs that feel confident, genuine, and personal.

> The experience is designed around comfort, privacy, expression, and professional in-camera capture.

Content may refer to:

- Lighting
- Posing
- Direction
- Expression
- Comfort
- Wardrobe preparation
- Privacy
- Boundaries
- Session planning
- Camera-side professional capture
- Careful image selection
- Studio environment
- Photographer guidance

## 3. Prohibited Terms and Concepts

The following terms, or substantially similar alternatives, must not be used in relation to client images or a client’s appearance:

- Retouch
- Retouched
- Retouching
- Edited
- Editing
- Professionally edited
- Professionally retouched
- Enhanced
- Enhancement
- Adjusted
- Adjustment
- Corrected
- Correction
- Polished
- Airbrushed
- Airbrushing
- Skin smoothing
- Smooth skin
- Body shaping
- Body reshaping
- Reshaping
- Touch-up
- Touch-ups
- Touchups
- Flattering edits
- Premium retouching
- Post-production
- Post production
- Image manipulation
- Digital alteration
- Altered images
- Removing imperfections
- Removing blemishes
- Improving appearance through editing
- Making someone look different
- Filters
- Softening features

## 4. Approved Alternatives

Use the following wording instead.

| Do not use | Use instead |
|---|---|
| Professionally retouched images | Selected images |
| Professionally edited images | Selected images |
| Edited gallery | Selected gallery |
| Final edited gallery | Final selected gallery |
| Premium retouching | Additional selected images or extra session time, where applicable |
| Flattering edits | Considered lighting, posing, and direction |
| Enhance your appearance | Capture you naturally and confidently |
| Correct imperfections | Focus on comfort, expression, lighting, and posing |

## 5. Approved Client-Facing Language

The following wording is approved for use across relevant content:

> All images are treated with care and discretion. Only the best, most flattering shots are selected.

> Every session is approached with care, privacy, and respect for the individual.

> The studio experience is guided at a comfortable pace, with support around posing, expression, lighting, and personal preferences.

> You will be guided throughout the session so that the photographs feel natural, confident, and aligned with your comfort level.

> The focus is on creating a relaxed environment and capturing genuine, considered photographs in camera.

> Your selected images are handled with discretion and shared through the studio’s private gallery process.

## 6. Boudoir, Maternity, Birthing and Personal-Confidence Content

For sessions involving personal confidence, intimacy, pregnancy, maternity, birthing, or sensitive personal milestones, copy must prioritise:

- Privacy
- Consent
- Comfort
- Respect
- Clear boundaries
- Client control
- Gentle direction
- A calm and considered environment
- Natural in-camera representation

### Approved example

> These sessions are private, respectful, and guided entirely around your comfort. The photographer will provide gentle direction with posing, lighting, and expression, while ensuring the experience remains relaxed and aligned with your preferences.

### Not approved

> We will enhance your confidence through professional retouching.

### Not approved

> Images can be adjusted to create your preferred final look.

## 7. Package and Deliverable Copy

Package descriptions must not describe image deliverables as edited or retouched.

### Not permitted

> Includes 15 professionally retouched images.

> Includes a fully edited online gallery.

> Premium retouching is available as an add-on.

### Approved

> Includes 15 selected images.

> Includes a private selected-image gallery.

> Additional selected images or extra session time may be available, depending on the package.

## 8. Privacy and Discretion

Privacy language is encouraged, provided it does not imply image alteration.

### Approved

> All images are treated with care and discretion. Only the best, most flattering shots are selected.

> Personal sessions are handled with strict privacy, respect, and discretion.

> Your gallery is shared through the studio’s private access process.

### Not approved

> Images are discreetly edited and retouched before delivery.

> We use post-production to ensure privacy and confidence.

## 9. Final Copy Review Requirement

Before any content is published, sent, uploaded, or approved for client use, it must be checked to ensure it does not:

1. Refer to editing, retouching, enhancement, adjustment, correction, alteration, or post-production changes to appearance.
2. Imply that a client will look different from how they appear in camera.
3. Describe deliverables as edited or retouched images.
4. Use prohibited terminology in packages, promotions, gallery descriptions, FAQs, booking content, or client communications.

Any content containing prohibited language must be rewritten before publication.
